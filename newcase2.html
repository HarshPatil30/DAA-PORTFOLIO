Healthcare Equipment Allocation

Optimizing the allocation of medical equipment across hospitals

Algorithms & Implementation

1. Priority Queues

Use Case: Prioritizing the allocation of medical equipment to critical patients based on urgency.

Explanation: Priority Queues help assign medical equipment to patients with the highest priority, ensuring that urgent cases receive the resources they need first.

2. Greedy Algorithm

Use Case: Minimizing resource wastage while allocating equipment.

Explanation: A Greedy Algorithm is used to make the best immediate choice for allocating equipment, aiming to minimize wastage or unused resources.

3. Dynamic Programming

Use Case: Optimizing equipment allocation over time, taking changing demand into account.

Explanation: Dynamic Programming helps optimize the distribution of medical resources over time, adjusting allocations as demand or supply fluctuates.

4. Dijkstra's Algorithm

Use Case: Finding the most efficient route for equipment delivery across multiple hospital departments.

Explanation: Dijkstra's Algorithm calculates the shortest and most efficient delivery routes for medical equipment, minimizing delivery time.

5. Floyd-Warshall Algorithm

Use Case: Determining optimal allocation paths between multiple hospital locations.

Explanation: Floyd-Warshall can be used to calculate all pairs of shortest paths for equipment allocation across multiple hospital locations.

SDGs

SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being

Optimizing patient appointment scheduling enhances patient flow, reduces wait times, and improves clinical outcomes. By ensuring timely care and better resource management, these efforts contribute directly to the goal of good health and well-being for all.

  • Indicator 3.8.1: Coverage of essential health services.
  • Indicator 3.9.1: Mortality rate attributed to chronic diseases (e.g., cardiovascular diseases, cancer).
  • Indicator 3.3.1: Number of new HIV infections per 1,000 uninfected population.
  • Indicator 3.9.2: Mortality rate attributed to unintentional poisoning.

SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

Streamlining appointment scheduling enhances the efficiency of healthcare staff, reducing administrative workloads and increasing productivity. This improved workforce productivity contributes to economic growth in the healthcare sector by optimizing resources, lowering costs, and improving care delivery.

  • Indicator 8.5.1: Employment rate for youth, women, and men.
  • Indicator 8.2.1: Annual growth rate of real GDP per employed person.
  • Indicator 8.5.2: Proportion of persons with disabilities who are employed.

SDG 10: Reduced Inequality

Improved patient appointment scheduling allows for self-scheduling and more effective time slot management, making healthcare more accessible to individuals from diverse backgrounds. The prioritization of critical cases ensures urgent care is not delayed, reducing healthcare disparities.

  • Indicator 10.2.1: Proportion of people living below 50% of median income.
  • Indicator 10.3.1: Proportion of countries that have implemented a law, policy, or program to ensure equal access to public services.
  • Indicator 10.7.1: Recruitment of migrants into employment opportunities, including health-related jobs, fostering better healthcare delivery.

SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

Implementing technological solutions such as AVL trees and priority queues for scheduling fosters innovation in healthcare infrastructure. It ensures better management of resources, reduces overcrowding, and improves the overall healthcare experience through the use of optimized technologies.

  • Indicator 9.1.1: Proportion of rural population with access to all-season roads (indirectly related to hospital access and infrastructure).
  • Indicator 9.4.1: CO2 emissions per unit of value added (relevant for reducing the carbon footprint of hospital operations through technological solutions).
  • Indicator 9.b.1: Proportion of individuals using the internet to access health services.

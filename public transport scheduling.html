Public Transport Scheduling

Introduction

Efficient public transport scheduling is key to enhancing mobility, reducing waiting times, and improving urban transit systems. Advanced algorithms and real-time data can transform the way public transport operates.

Key Implementations

Dynamic Scheduling with Real-Time Data

Use dynamic programming to adjust bus and train schedules based on real-time demand and traffic conditions. This minimizes delays and ensures optimal resource utilization.

Route Optimization

Leverage Dijkstra's algorithm to find the shortest and most efficient routes for public transport vehicles, reducing travel time and fuel consumption.

Demand Forecasting

Use machine learning models to predict passenger demand based on historical data and trends. Accurate forecasts allow better allocation of vehicles and resources.

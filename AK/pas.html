Patient Appointment Scheduling

Introduction

Optimized patient appointment scheduling ensures smooth operations by improving patient flow, reducing wait times, and enhancing the patient experience. By making the scheduling process more efficient, healthcare facilities can reduce overcrowding, provide timely care, and use resources more effectively. Using technology for scheduling also helps avoid mistakes and improves staff efficiency, leading to higher patient satisfactions and better clinical outcomes.

Cases and Their Implementation

Patient Prioritization and Emergency Handling

Problem: Ensuring that urgent and critical cases are addressed promptly without disrupting regular schedules.

Approach: Priority queue (max-heap)

Implement a priority queue (max-heap) to manage patient data. Patients are assigned priorities based on urgency, with critical cases served first. This ensures fair and efficient handling of emergencies.

Appointment Scheduling and Slot Management

Problem: Efficiently managing time slots and resolving conflicts to avoid double-booking.

Approach: AVL Tree, Segment Tree

Use an AVL Tree for slot allocation and a Segment Tree to detect and resolve overlapping appointments.

Patient Self-Scheduling and Coordination

Problem: Authorize patients to schedule their own appointments while ensuring seamless coordination between patients and providers.

Approach: AVL Tree, Hash maps

Develop a self-service portal where patients can book appointments online. Use a hash map to track and match provider availability with patient preferences.

Appointment Confirmation and Post-Scheduling Activities

Problem: Reducing no-shows, managing cancellations, and ensuring an uninterrupted post-scheduling process.

Approach: Priority queue, Fenwick Trees

Send automated reminders using a queue for notification pipelines and handle cancellations with a priority queue, re-allocating freed slots to waitlisted patients using a priority queue.

Optimizing Hospital Bed Allocation

Introduction

Efficient hospital bed management is critical to ensuring timely and adequate healthcare for patients. In hospitals with fluctuating patient numbers and varying levels of care required, managing available beds and optimizing their allocation becomes a significant challenge. This case focuses on developing a system that streamlines hospital bed availability tracking, patient prioritization, bed reservation, and inter-hospital coordination, ensuring that resources are used optimally.

Use Cases

1. Bed Availability and Management

Task: To track hospital bed availability in different hospitals, update occupancy, and support patient admissions and transfers.

SDG: 3

Problem Tool Used: Hash Map Data Structure

This use case provides the ability to query available beds for specific hospital departments and assists in identifying hospitals with available beds of a certain type, facilitating patient transfers or admissions when necessary.

2. Bed Allocation

Task: To manage bed availability, prioritize patients using a queue, and allocate beds to the highest priority patients first.

SDG: 3

Problem Tool Used: Priority Queue and Hash Map

The Bed Allocation System aims to allocate hospital beds to patients based on their priority level (High, Medium, Low) and available capacity in different departments (e.g., ICU, General, Emergency).
